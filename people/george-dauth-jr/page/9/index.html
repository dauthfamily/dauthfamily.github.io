1930-05-30 - Greeley Daily Tribune - June Dauth in Command

1930-05-30 – Greeley Daily Tribune – June Dauth in Command
June Dauth in command of the National Guard Firing Squad during the Memorial Day Parade in Greeley

Transcription
Co. M Softball Team To Pllay Brush Wed.
"Company M, local unit of the Colorado National Guard, will throw its softball team into the breach against Brush town team Wednesday evening at 6:15-o’clock in a game to be played at Island Grove park. The game is free and pubic is invited to attend. The local unit is getting in shape for the state softball tournament which will be held Labor Day, Sept. 1, at Lamar. Division tournaments will be held the middle of next month with Company M playing at Loveland. Another division meet will be held at Brush. Company M is looked upon as having the fastest outfit in Greeley and was counted upon to win the city league had it not been necessary to forfeit games while the group was at encampment in Golden. George Dauth, Jr., is manager of the outfit and George Irvin is commanding officer of the military unit. The team is anxious to schedule any practice games possible and will meet all comers from now until the division tournament."

1929-11-07 - The Mirror - June Dauth Second Lieutenant

1929-11-07 – The Mirror – June Dauth Second Lieutenant

Transcription
Teachers Students Are National Guardsmen
"Company “M”, 157th Infantry of the Colorado National Guard, located at Greeley, Colo., is composed mostly of Colorado Teachers College men, according to Dick Hobson, top sergeant of the company. The outfit a machine gun company, drills every Tuesday night at 7:30 at the armory on Eight avenue. The drills consist of squad and company movements, machine gun drills and tactics, and pistol drill. The gun range, located seventeen miles east of Greeley, is owned by the United States government and is maintained for the exclusive use of guardsmen. George Irvin, of the extension department, is the captain and George Dauth, Jr. is second lieutenant of the company."

1929-06-18 - The Mirror - June and Leslie Dauth Join National Guard

1929-06-18 – The Mirror – June and Leslie Dauth Join National Guard
June Dauth Second Lieutenant, Leslie Dauth Sergeant in National Guard

Transcription
Guard Claims Many Students for Two Weeks
"College Men Belonging to National Guard Are Encamped Near Golden; Young man, join the National Guard," must be the latest interpretation of Horace Greeley's famous words. That is, to judge from the great number of college men who are in training with the two Greeley companies at the state rifle range near Golden, Colorado. Two full coaches and an over-loaded baggage car gives ample proof that college men cater to military life. The troops left Greeley Saturday, June 8, to stay until June 23. Men who are at present or have been enrolled at Teachers College and are now at Golden: Captain George Irvin, Second Lieutenant George Dauth, Jr., First Sergeant Charles E. Hobson, Sergeants Leslie Dauth, Oscar R. Romine, William H. Fitzgerald, Reece P. Hopkins. Corporals Charles E. Reeder, Paul V. McKinley. Privates Roy A. Newkirk, Bob E. Blake, Robert E. Byrns, Lonis"

1926-04-01 - The Mirror - June Dauth Receives Basketball Trophy

1926-04-01 – The Mirror – June Dauth Receives Basketball Trophy

Transcription
Six Gold Basketballs Presented to Cage Champs Last Evening by Cooper at Letter-mens's Meeting
"In appreciation of the championship honors which the 1926 basketball squad brought to Colorado Teachers College, Coach George E. Cooper presented to six players at a meeting of Bear letter-men last night small gold basketballs. The emblems are the gift of the college. Last year the championship baseball team received gold baseballs presented by Ed Boelter, prominent sport fan among Greeley business men. The Bear cagers who received the gold balls last night were: Captain George Glidden, Lester Piper, Osborne Johnson, Pete Brown, Jack Mashburn, and George Dauth, Jr."
