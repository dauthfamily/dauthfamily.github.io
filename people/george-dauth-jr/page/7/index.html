1954-10-02 - Greeley Daily Tribune - June Dauth Newspaper Boy

1954-10-02 – Greeley Daily Tribune – June Dauth Newspaper Boy

Transcription
Greeley Tribune Carriers at Office Some 34 Years Ago
"Some of the Greeley Daily Tribune carriers in 1920 are assembled here in front of the office when it occupied all the north part of the ground floor of the Camfield hotel. Present-day newspaper boys are honored on Saturday, Newspaper Boys day. Mrs. E. E. (Margaret) Lewis, who was in charge of the carriers, can be very dimly seen in the doorway. In the front row, left to right: Bruce Harbottle, Floyd Allen, Albert Luther, William K. (Bus) Penrose, Stanly A Knapp, and Bill Condon. The back row, left to right: George Dauth Jr., Wilbur Benton, Harold Jones, John Bethell, Lawton Luther, Ed Coit Johnson, a youthful Mr. Peterson whose first name could not be learned; and Bill Grove. George Dauth Jr., a Greeley high school and Colorado state football and basketball star, died when was 27 years old. He was working for his father in the grocery and market. He was a brother of Leslie Dauth, Greeley realtor. Wilbur Benton farms near Lucerne. Harold Jones is head of the Jones Tent and Awning company here. John Bothell is now studying for his doctorate at Peabody Institute at Haysville, Tenn. When he receives his degree he will return to Colorado State College of Education to teach. Lawton Luther has an executive position with the government Printing Office in Washington, Colt Johnson is the Culligan’s soft water service at LaSalle. Bill Grove majored in industrial arts, especially in woodworking at Colorado Sate here. He taught in Estes Park for some time. Now he operates the Eastern Furniture company at Estes Park, which manufactures most of its own furniture. Bruce Harbottle, for some time an accountant with the Shell Oil company in California., is now in New York City with the same company. Floyd Allen is the Plymouth dealer at Eaton. Albert Luther has an executive position among the estimators for the government printing office in Washington. Listed among the carrier at this time but not in the picture are: M. E. H. Smith of Greeley, the present district attorney of the eight Judicial district; Bradford hatch, a pressman at the Denver Post; and Ned Hamilton, now an appliance salesman at Oakland Calif. Thirty-four years ago all Tribune carrier routes were in Greeley. Now the Tribune has 36 carriers in Greeley and 12 in county towns outside Greeley. The number of Greeley readers has more than doubled."

1932-06-02 - The Lyons Mirror - June Dauth Obituary

1932-06-02 – The Lyons Mirror – June Dauth Obituary

Transcription
SON OF FORMER LYONS RESIDENT, PASSES AWAY
"In a letter form Mrs. Charles O. Swanson of Greeley, Colorado, to Mrs. Willard Robbins, she enclosed an article of the death of a son of Mr. and Mrs. George Dauth. Mrs. Dauth was formerly Floy Yeaton, a daughter of Mr. and Mrs. Lote Yeaton of Lyons, and lied here when a girl. This is the fifth death in her family and she has only one son living out of a family of six. Many girlhood friends here will sympathize with Mrs. Dauth in her sorrow. George Dauth Jr., 27, son of Mr. and Mrs. George Dauth of Greeley, died at 2:30 o’clock Wednesday morning at the Greeley hospital, his death coming suddenly after apparent excellent progress. It was hoped to take him home this week, and he was in high spirits Tuesday night. Young Dauth had been in the hospital for about a month, having undergone an operation for appendicitis. Pneumonia developed a week later, then a blood clot and “milk” leg threatened his life. He improved afterwards and it was believed he would recover, when blood clot on his lung caused his death. Dauth was graduated form Greeley high school in 1924, after an active four years of athletic participation. He was center on the 1922 state basketball championship team of the high school and played all through his high school career. He was all conference guard in football one year. He attended teachers college for two years and was a forward on the team when Teachers won the conference championship. He has lately been in Baab and Dauth grocery and market. Dauth was first lieutenant in the National Guard here, and for nine consecutive years had attended the summer camp, one of the tow or three men who had that record. He was a member of the Greeley Elks Club. Besides his parents, Dauth is survived by one brother, Leslie Dauth. Funeral services will be at 2:00 o’clock on Friday afternoon at Macy’s drawing room. Interment at Linn Grove. Elks’ service will be at Macy’s; burial will be military."

1932-05-19 – The Daily Sentinel – June Dauth Obituary

Transcription
SYMPATHY EXPRESSED OVER THE DEATH OF NATL GUARD OFFICER
"Denver. May 19. – Col. W. C. Danks, adjutant general of the Colorado National Guard today issued a formal order expressing the sympathy of the state military department to the friends and relatives of First Lieutenant George A. Dauth Jr. of Greeley who died in a Greeley hospital from a complication of diseases. Lieut. Dauth enlisted in 1923 as a private and had gone through all grades of promotion to the rank he held at his death. He was attached to Company M, 157th Infantry."

1932-05-19 - Greeley Daily Tribune - June Dauth Funeral

1932-05-19 – Greeley Daily Tribune – June Dauth Funeral

Transcription
Funeral Service for "June" Dauth Friday
"Pall bearers for George Dauth Jr., at the funeral Friday afternoon, 2 o'clock at Macy's, will be Harold Jones, Barton Meeker, August Baab, Maurice Burbridge, Joe James and Merle Ogle. Honorary pall bearers, announcing the death of Dauth; were issued Wednesday by W.C. Danks, adjutant general of Colorado. The orders state: "Lieutenant Dauth entered the service of the Colorado National Guard as a private in Headquarters company, Third Battalion, 157th infantry, on Nov. 6. 1923, where he served efficiently in the grades of corporal, sergeant and staff sergeant until May 25, 1927, when he was discharged to accept a commission as second lieutenant. He served as second lieutenant in the same organization from May, 25, 1927, until Feb. 7, 1930; when he received his promotion to first lieutenant, in which grade he was serving at the time of his death. In the passing of Lieutenant Dauth, the National Guard loses a most enthusiastic and energetic young officer, and this office extends to his relatives and host of friends most sincere sympathy."
