1932-06-02 - The Lyons Mirror - June Dauth Obituary

1932-06-02 – The Lyons Mirror – June Dauth Obituary

Transcription
SON OF FORMER LYONS RESIDENT, PASSES AWAY
"In a letter form Mrs. Charles O. Swanson of Greeley, Colorado, to Mrs. Willard Robbins, she enclosed an article of the death of a son of Mr. and Mrs. George Dauth. Mrs. Dauth was formerly Floy Yeaton, a daughter of Mr. and Mrs. Lote Yeaton of Lyons, and lied here when a girl. This is the fifth death in her family and she has only one son living out of a family of six. Many girlhood friends here will sympathize with Mrs. Dauth in her sorrow. George Dauth Jr., 27, son of Mr. and Mrs. George Dauth of Greeley, died at 2:30 o’clock Wednesday morning at the Greeley hospital, his death coming suddenly after apparent excellent progress. It was hoped to take him home this week, and he was in high spirits Tuesday night. Young Dauth had been in the hospital for about a month, having undergone an operation for appendicitis. Pneumonia developed a week later, then a blood clot and “milk” leg threatened his life. He improved afterwards and it was believed he would recover, when blood clot on his lung caused his death. Dauth was graduated form Greeley high school in 1924, after an active four years of athletic participation. He was center on the 1922 state basketball championship team of the high school and played all through his high school career. He was all conference guard in football one year. He attended teachers college for two years and was a forward on the team when Teachers won the conference championship. He has lately been in Baab and Dauth grocery and market. Dauth was first lieutenant in the National Guard here, and for nine consecutive years had attended the summer camp, one of the tow or three men who had that record. He was a member of the Greeley Elks Club. Besides his parents, Dauth is survived by one brother, Leslie Dauth. Funeral services will be at 2:00 o’clock on Friday afternoon at Macy’s drawing room. Interment at Linn Grove. Elks’ service will be at Macy’s; burial will be military."

1954-10-28 - Obituary of Florence Dauth

1954-10-28 – Obituary of Florence Dauth

Transcription
Mrs. Florence Dauth, 76, Died
"Mrs. Florence May Dauth, 76, a resident of Greeley since 1900, died Thursday morning at the Weld County Nursing home. She had been a patient there since Dec. 15. Mrs. Dauth's home was at 1731 Eleventh avenue. Her son, Leslie L. Dauth, lives at 1113 Eighteenth street. Born Sept. 15, 1878 at Bancroft, Neb., Mrs. Dauth operated a millinery store, where J. V. Smith and Son is located, when she first came to Greeley. She was married to George Dauth Sept. 3 1903 in Denver. He was killed in 1946. Mrs. Dauth was a member of Park Congregational church. Beside her son, she is survived by two grandchildren, Leslie Ann and Jerry D. Dauth, both of Greeley; a sister, Mrs. Ivy Lewis of Greeley; and two brothers, Bert Yeaton of Dallas, Tex., and Alfred Yeaton of El Reno, Okla. Funeral services will be held at 10 o'clock Saturday morning from Macy's drawing room, with interment at Linn Grove. Friends may make memorial donations to the Cancer fund in lieu of flowers."

c. 1946 - Florence Dauth Housemother of ASA

c. 1946 – Florence Dauth Housemother of ASA
Article for the ASA Sorority House with Florence Dauth as Housemother. The ASA Sorority was affiliated with the Colorado State College of Education (University of Northern Colorado). The Phoenix was an annual publication put out by the Alpha Sigma Alpha sorority.

Transcription
Beta Beta's Castle
""In our castle on the avenue" is where you'll find Beta Beta's most of the time. The ASA house is a block from the C.S.C.E. campus and is situated on an avenue. The house has been part of our chapter for many years. During that time Mrs. Dauth, owner of the house, has been housemother and much more too, for she is really a foster mother to the whole sorority. Our castle itself is a two-story frame building with a large front porch. Upstairs there are three double rooms and one single bedroom. Downstairs there is a double bedroom and a large living room and dining room. The single upstairs room has earned the title, "Bridal Suite," because most of the girls who have moved into the room have become engaged shortly afterwards. Needless to say there is a long list of applicants for the room. There are six other sororities at C.S.C.E. Ours is the only one which has a private house. The others have been meeting at dormitories but recently they were banned from residence halls. What envious eyes admire our home! What we like best about our house is its versatility. On Monday nights it is a meeting place for all of us. During rushing it serves as a lovely home for an afternoon tea, a lounge for an informal card party, a castle for our Cinderella banquet. Upon occasion it has been a tavern, a tropical isle, the big tent and once it was The Sad Shack, hangout for mountain folk. The outside has often been changed for homecoming contests. Last year it sported a big brown bear which won second prize. Our house is the scene of pledging, installation, and active services. In it we have often experienced the thrill that comes when one of us passes the candy and the whole sorority joins in the Sweetheart song. On its porch we have stood while the lucky fellow and his frat serenade our sweetheart. Our house is the place where we have our happiest times, do our most serious work, and plan for the welfare of our group. Our being Alpha Sigmas means much to all of us at Beta Beta, and when we think of the sorority we think of our house and how it is truly our castle on the avenue."

1953-08-22 - Greeley Tribune - Yeaton Sibling Reunion

1953-08-22 – Greeley Tribune – Yeaton Sibling Reunion
The Yeaton siblings posing for a photo for their first reunion in 53 years.

Transcription
Together for First Time in 53 Years
"For the first time in 53 years the brothers and sister pictured above are together. They are, from left to right, Bert S. Yeaton of Dallas, Texas, rs. George Dauth of 1731 Eleventh avenue, Mrs. J. A. Lewis of 1325 Eleventh avenue and Alfred A. Yeaton of El reno, Okla. The reunion marked the first time in 27 years that Mrs. Dauth had seen her brother Alfred and the first time in 51 years that Mrs. Lewis had seen him. The brothers came to Greeley directly from a visit to their old family home in Lyons, Neb. The four are the children of the late Mr. and Mrs. Zelotus D. Yeaton of Lyons. Photo by Leslie L. Dauth, son of Mrs. George Dauth."
