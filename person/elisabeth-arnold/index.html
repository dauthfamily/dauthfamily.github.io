Elisabeth Arnold

Elisabeth Arnold was likely born near Westhofen, Germany in the 1820s. She married Andreas Dauth, who was also likely from the Westhofen area, around 1850.1Dauth Family Archive. “c. 1914-1940 – Andreas Dauth Family Tree – Page 4.” Dauth Family Archive, 15 Feb. 2023, https://dauthfamily.github.io/document/c-1914-1940-andreas-dauth-family-tree-page-4/. Accessed 29 Mar. 2023. Elisabeth and Andreas had their first daughter, Elisabeth “Elsie”, on April 16, 1851 and first son Ludwig “Louis”, on November 14, 1852.2Dauth Family Archive. “Elisabeth Dauth,” February 10, 2023. Accessed March 29, 2023. https://dauthfamily.github.io/person/elisabeth-dauth/.3Dauth Family Archive. “Louis Dauth,” June 5, 2022. Accessed March 29, 2023. https://dauthfamily.github.io/person/louis-dauth/. Another daughter Karoline “Lina” was born June 23, 1857. Elisabeth and Andreas went on to have seven more children, Eva Katherine “Katie” on June 4th 1864, Philip in September of 1866, and Georg “George” on October 24th 1868.4Dauth Family Archive. “Eva Katherine Dauth,” March 11, 2023. Accessed March 29, 2023. https://dauthfamily.github.io/person/eva-katherine-dauth/.5Dauth Family Archive. “Philip Dauth,” June 14, 2022. Their four other daughters, Julia, Anna, Marguerite, and Wilhemina have unknown birth information.7Dauth Family Archive. “Julia Dauth,” February 15, 2023. Elisabeth spent her time taking care of the home and teaching the children how to cook.11Dauth Family Archive. “c. 1980s – Isabella Fraser Family History Notes – Page 18 – Family History By Eva Fitting.” Dauth Family Archive, 30 May. 2022, https://dauthfamily.github.io/document/elizabeth-fitting-family-history-page-18/. Accessed 29 Mar. 2023.

After their son Louis was wounded in the Franco-Prussian War of 1870, Elisabeth and Andreas encouraged their children to immigrate to America to escape any future conflicts.12Dauth Family Archive. “c. 1914-1940 – George Dauth Notes On Dauth Family Tree,” May 30, 2022. Accessed March 29, 2023. https://dauthfamily.github.io/document/george-dauth-notes-on-dauth-family-tree/. Their eldest daughter Elsie had already emigrated in 1869.13Dauth Family Archive. “Elisabeth Dauth,” February 10, 2023. Accessed March 29, 2023. https://dauthfamily.github.io/person/elisabeth-dauth/. She was followed by Louis in 1872.14Dauth Family Archive. “Louis Dauth,” June 5, 2022. Accessed March 29, 2023. https://dauthfamily.github.io/person/louis-dauth/. At some time prior to 1883, Elisabeth and Andreas moved to Mainz, Germany where a brother of either Andreas or Elisabeth owned a lumberyard “half a block from the Rhine river.”23Dauth Family Archive. “1883-01-11 – Fort Collins Courier – Germany Floods,” May 30, 2022.
