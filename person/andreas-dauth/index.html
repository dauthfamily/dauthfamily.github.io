Andreas Dauth

Andreas Dauth was likely born near Westhofen, Germany in the 1830s. He married Elisabeth Arnold who was also likely from the Westhofen area. Andreas and Elisabeth had their first daughter, Elsie "Elisabeth", on April 16, 1851 and first son Ludwig “Louis”, on November 14, 1852. Another daughter Caroline “Lena” was born June 23, 1857. Soon after, in 1858, Andreas began teaching at the local school. Andreas and Elisabeth went on to have seven more children, Juliana on February 3rd 1861, Eva Katherine “Katie” on June 4th 1864, Phillip in September of 1866, and George on October 24th 1868. The three others daughters, Anna, Marguerite, and Wilhemina have unknown birth information. In 1871, Lena recalled that Andreas retired from teaching and later became the town postmaster.

After their son Louis was conscripted into the Franco-Prussian War of 1870, Andreas and Elisabeth encouraged their children to immigrate to America to escape any future violence. Elisabeth was the first to leave in 1869, followed by Louis in 1872. They were followed by their brother Philip in 1882. Caroline came in 1883. Katie and George immigrated in 1884. Juliana, Anna, Marguerite, and Wilhemina stayed in Germany.

At some time prior to 1883, Andreas and Elisabeth moved to Mainz (Mayence), where a brother of either Andreas or Elisabeth owned a lumberyard “half a block from the Rhine river.”
