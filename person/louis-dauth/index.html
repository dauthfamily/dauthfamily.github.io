Louis Dauth

Ludwig “Louis” Dauth was born in Westhofen, Germany on November 14, 1852 to Andreas and Elisabeth Dauth. Louis was conscripted into the North German Confederation in 1870, at the age of 18, to fight for the Prussian Army during the Franco Prussian War. As a male nurse he participated in “nearly every one of the hard fought battles of that short but bloody war” and was injured on the battlefield with a bullet to his leg. He was awarded two medals for his service, “one of steel, the other of brass” one of which was the Iron Cross. After the war ended he spent a year at home before immigrating to America in 1872.

He spent several months on the Eastern coast, before moving west to Colorado and locating to Denver in 1873. Louis worked a variety of jobs while staying first at 388 Larimer and then boarding at the Swiss House. He worked as a Clerk in 1874, Waiter at the American House in 1875, and Bar Keeper in 1876. In 1877 Louis partnered with John Reither to create Dauth & Reithers Cabinet Makers and Upholsterers. The furniture business wasn’t agreeing with Louis and so he took his chance with a move to Fort Collins in October 1877 upon completion of the Colorado Central railroad to Cheyenne. He came to Fort Collins with just $250 in capital and used it to start a bakery “in a small one-story frame building” on the Mosman Block. Soon afterwards Louis started a business partnership with M. M. Zimmerman to create the Dauth & Zimmerman Bakery, located one door down from of the southwest corner of Linden and Jefferson. The partnership lasted just a few months until March 1878. August of 1878 Louis purchased the building three doors down from of the southwest corner of Linden and Jefferson known was the “New Store” and repurposed the building with an oven and sales room, turning it into a bakery. During the next two years Louis ran the bakery and became the Vice Grand of the Independent Order of Odd Fellows at Collins Lodge #19.

On December 10 1879 Louis married Flora Herrendoerfer. They were married at the German Methodist church in Denver, Colorado. After their marriage they moved to 310 South Howes Street in Fort Collins. In March of 1880 Louis partnered with E.W. Reed to purchase and develop a business lot across the street from Louis’s store which would be known as the Reed-Dauth Block, now 223 Linden Street. The building was completed December of 1880 and Louis moved his bakery into the building renaming it to the “Louis Bakery and Confectionery.” In March 1881 Louis and Flora had their first child Walter. In April 1883 Louis became a town Alderman, specializing on the towns street work. Louis continued to run his bakery independently until June 21 1883 when A. E. Annis purchased half the business renaming it to Dauth & Annis. On January 8th 1883 Louis’s daughter Louisa “Lulu” was born. The bakery partnership lasted until February 5th 1885 when A.E. Annis retired. Louis continued to run the store independently under his own name until February 16th 1888 when Louis decided to move his business to Denver. He sold his business and home to the Bennet Brothers.

Louis and Flora moved to Five Points, Denver, first living at 2424 Glenarm and then 2424 Lincoln in 1889. Louis established his new grocery at 2458 Washington Avenue, eventually expanding the business to 2452, 2454, and 2456 Washington Avenue. On November 17 1889 Louis’s daughter Lulu passed away from diphtheria. The family then moved to 2524 Clarkson in 1891. In April 1891 Louis’s daughter Louise and son Herman were born. In 1893 Louis expanded his grocery to include a Saloon. In 1894 the family moved to 10 25th Avenue. Louis’s brother George moved in with the family and worked as a butcher at the grocery. Louis’s son Walter began working as a clerk in 1899. In 1901 Louis’s daughter Helen was born.

In August of 1902 Louis’s wife Flora passed away. In 1903 Louis’s son Walter passed away. In 1905 Louis’s brother Phillip and his wife Katie both tragically died, leaving behind five children: Isabella, Louise, Phillip, Fred, and Elsie. George and Florence Dauth adopted Louise, Fred, and Elsie; Caroline and Jacob Fitting adopted Phillip, while it’s likely Louis adopted Isabella.

On November 14 1905 Louis married Bertha Zweifel and took in Bertha’s daughter Irene Eyser. From 1905 to 1911 Louis was involved with the local German Krieger Verein, working as the organizations Treasurer and Secretary. From 1915 to 1929 Louis began selling dry goods and hardware, while Bertha began to sell Notions in her own part of the store.

Louis Dauth passed away on December 22nd 1944. He died at the age of 92 and was buried at Fairmount Cemetery in Denver.
