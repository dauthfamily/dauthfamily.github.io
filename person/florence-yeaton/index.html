Florence May Yeaton

Florence May Yeaton was born in Bancraft, Nebraska on September 15th, 1878 to Zelotes and Emily Yeaton. In 1900, at the age of 21, she moved to Denver Colorado to work as a milliner. In 1901 Florence met George Dauth, who was working as a clerk at local grocery store. They married on September 8th 1903 and moved to 825 6th Street in Greeley. On September 10th, 1903, Florence announced the opening of her millinery parlor, "The Parlor Millinery" , located at 702 Ninth Avenue, Greeley. A month later Florence made plans to move her millinery to the first floor of 806 9th Street. Florence would sell her millinery parlor to Mrs. Nelson Demorest on June 2nd 1904 in anticipation of the birth of her first child, George Jr., on September 14th 1904.

In 1904, Florence's brother-in-law, Philip Dauth, and sister-in-law, Katie Dauth, both tragically died, leaving behind five children: Isabella, Louise, Phillip, Fred, and Elsie. Florence and George adopted Louise, Fred, and Elsie while George’s other siblings cared for Isabella and Phillip.

Florence and George had their second child, Elizabeth, on June 3rd 1906. In 1907 they moved to their second home at 1329 7th Street. Their third child, Leslie, was born on June 5th 1908. On December 3rd 1910 they had twins who died in infancy. In 1913 they had a third son, Donald, who also died in infancy. In 1924 Florence’s daughter Elizabeth became ill, passing away on July 20th 1924.

Florence was active in many of Greeley's social clubs, including the "Unique Card Club", "The Rebekahs", the "North Ward Mother's Circle", the "Vincina Club", and the "Kensington Club." In 1929, when her sons were enrolled at the Colorado Teachers College, she started entertaining the college sororities at her summer home "Idlewild Lodge." On October 3rd, 1929, Florence began renting 1731 Eleventh Avenue to the Sigma Upsilon Sorority and became their House Mother. Florence would be a House Mother for almost two decades for the various sororities that rented this property.

On April 15th 1932 Florence's oldest son, June, became ill after appendicitis surgery, and passed away on May 18th 1932. In 1937 Florence and George moved to 1731 11th Avenue, next door to their son Leslie and his family. On April 29th 1946, Florence's husband George was struck by a locomotive and killed. In her later years Florence spent her time as House Mother of the local sororities, entertaining guests at "Idlewild Lodge", and being a member of the Park Congregational Church.

Florence passed away on October 28th 1956 at the age of 76. She was buried at Linn Grove Cemetery in Greeley.
