Elizabeth Dauth

Elizabeth Dauth was born in Greeley, Colorado on June 2, 1906 to George and Florence Dauth. She was the family's second born and only daughter of the George Dauth family. She was likely named after George's mother Elisabeth, or George's late older sister Elizabeth.

Elizabeth began attending Greeley High School in 1921, where she quickly became involved with a number of social clubs including the B. B. Club, K. K. Klub, and Girls Association. From 1922 to 1923 she played on the Greeley High School tennis team. She had a passion for photography and provided many of her photos for use in the Spud yearbook.

In 1923 Elizabeth began attending Colorado Teachers College where she became a member of the Delta Sigma Epsilon sorority. The following year she was elected as Vice President of the sorority. In July of 1924 she became seriously ill, and passed away unexpectedly on July 20th, 1924. She had just turned 18 years old.
